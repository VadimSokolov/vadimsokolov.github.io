Program Overview

The MS in AI prepares students to work across the full AI pipeline — from building models to deploying them in production to governing their use. The curriculum spans machine learning, deep learning, planning, AI systems engineering, ethics, and IT management.

  • Design and train ML and deep learning models
  • Build and deploy AI systems on cloud, edge, and embedded platforms
  • Evaluate AI systems against risk and fairness frameworks
  • Make decisions under uncertainty using reinforcement learning and planning algorithms
  • Translate technical AI work into business and policy context

The program is jointly offered by faculty from Computer Science, Statistics, Operations Research, Cybersecurity, Public Policy, and Business across multiple GMU colleges.

Technically Inclined Graduates

  • Recent BS or MS graduates from any quantitative or technical discipline — CS, engineering, economics, mathematics, physics, or similar
  • This is a technical program: you will write code, work with math, and build systems
  • No CS degree required, but you should be comfortable with quantitative reasoning
  • Full-time option: complete in 1.5 years

Curriculum

The program consists of 30 credit hours: 18 credits of core coursework and 12 credits of electives.

Core Courses (18 credits)

Course Description
AII 600: Foundations and Practice of Machine Learning Analyze datasets, implement ML solutions, evaluate against risk frameworks
AII 601: Planning and Decision Making for Intelligent Agents MDPs, reinforcement learning, policy gradient algorithms, search-based planning
AII 602: Foundations and Practice of Deep Learning Neural networks, computer vision, NLP, generative AI, explainability
AII 603: Engineering AI Systems and Pipelines Build and deploy large-scale AI on edge, cloud, and embedded platforms. Team-based projects
GBUS 662: Management of IT Strategic and managerial aspects of IT assets and organizational value
ME 576: AI — Ethics, Policy, and Society Transparency, fairness, bias, privacy, surveillance, responsible governance

Electives (12 credits)

The remaining 12 credits let you shape the degree around your career goals. Electives span AI policy, advanced machine learning, secure AI infrastructure, cybersecurity, health AI, and more. See the official catalog for the full list of available courses.

Sample schedule — A full-time student taking 3 courses per semester can complete the program in 3 semesters (1.5 years). Part-time students taking 1–2 courses per semester typically finish in 2–2.5 years.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • Is this an in-person program? For the 2025–2026 academic year, the program is offered in-person at the Fairfax campus. Some individual courses may be offered online. A fully online option may be available in the future.

  • Can I work full-time while enrolled? Yes. The program is designed for working professionals. All courses are offered in the evenings (4:30–7:10pm and 7:20–10pm).

  • How long does it take to complete? Full-time (3 courses/semester): approximately 1.5 years. Part-time (1–2 courses/semester): approximately 2–2.5 years. Note that some courses are offered only once per year.

  • I don’t have a CS degree. Can I succeed? Yes. Students come from computer science, engineering, cybersecurity, IT, mathematics, and other technical backgrounds. The pre-semester assessment identifies any gaps, and we provide free prep materials. The first core course (AII 600) builds machine learning foundations for students across backgrounds.
